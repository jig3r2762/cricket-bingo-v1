# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-06*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Cricket Bingo is a daily cricket knowledge challenge where fans match players to a grid based on stats, teams, and achievements.

**What it does:** Cricket Bingo presents players with a 3×3 or 4×4 grid of cricket categories (teams, countries, roles, stats, trophies). Each day you get a fresh deck of cricket players and must place them on the correct cells. Streaks, scoring multipliers, and a real-time leaderboard keep it competitive.

**Product category:** Daily sports trivia / knowledge challenge game (same shelf as Wordle, Immaculate Grid, MLB Pickle)

**Product type:** Free-to-play web game (mobile-responsive, Capacitor-ready for native app)

**Business model:** Currently free. Potential monetization: ads, premium features, sponsored grids, custom leagues/tournaments.

**Age range:** 18–40 — digitally native cricket fans who play Wordle-style games daily

**Primary use case:** A quick daily ritual to test cricket knowledge, compete with friends, and maintain a streak

**Jobs to be done:**
- "Give me a fun daily cricket challenge I can finish in 5 minutes"
- "Let me prove I know cricket better than my friends"
- "Keep me engaged with cricket even on non-match days"

**Use cases:**
- Morning commute daily game
- Office/group competition via leaderboard
- Cricket trivia nights / fan communities
- Fantasy cricket enthusiasts staying sharp on player stats

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Cricket fans have no daily, gamified way to test their knowledge. Generic sports trivia apps aren't cricket-specific enough, and cricket quiz apps are static and boring.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic trivia apps (Kahoot, QuizUp) — not cricket-specific, no daily fresh content
- Fantasy cricket (Dream11, MPL) — requires money, focused on prediction not knowledge
- Cricket news apps — passive consumption, no engagement or competition
- Social media cricket polls — no scoring, no persistence, no leaderboard

**What it costs them:** Boredom during non-match days; no way to engage with cricket knowledge socially

**Emotional tension:** "I know cricket deeply but there's no place to prove it daily without gambling money"

**Indirect:**
- Dream11 / fantasy cricket — money-based, prediction-focused
- ESPN Cricinfo quizzes — static, not daily, no social/leaderboard
- Instagram cricket polls — no persistence, no skill tracking

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Only daily cricket grid game in the market
- 3600+ real player database with accurate stats, teams, countries, roles, trophies
- Real-time daily leaderboard — compete globally, not just against yourself
- Both IPL and international cricket coverage
- Streak system with fire badges — daily habit loop
- Solvability-guaranteed grids — never get stuck on an impossible puzzle
- Admin panel — curated special grids for big match days, tournaments

**How we do it differently:** Unlike static quizzes, every game is a dynamic placement puzzle — you must think about which player fits which cell, not just recall facts.

**Why that's better:** Higher engagement, replayability, social competition, and satisfying "aha" moments when you find the perfect player for a tough combo cell.

**Why users choose us:** No other product exists that combines cricket knowledge, daily challenge format, and real-time competition.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't know enough cricket stats to play" | The game is approachable — you know players by team and country, which covers most cells. Harder stat cells are the bonus challenge. |
| "I'll just play once and get bored" | Daily grid resets, streak tracking, and leaderboard competition create a daily habit loop like Wordle. |
| "Why sign in with Google?" | Leaderboard and streak tracking require an account. It's one tap with Google — no passwords. |
| "The player I want isn't in the database" | We have 3600+ players and actively expand. Missing players can be requested. |

**Anti-persona:** Casual non-cricket fans, people who don't follow IPL or international cricket at all

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Cricket fans are bored between match days, tired of passive news consumption, want interactive engagement

**Pull:** Daily habit, streak streak rewards, leaderboard bragging rights, the satisfaction of nailing a tough combo cell

**Habit:** Some users already have morning Wordle/quiz routines — Cricket Bingo slots in alongside

**Anxiety:** "Will I embarrass myself on the leaderboard?" → Addressed by: no public wrong-answer display, individual score focus

**Words to avoid:** gambling, betting, quiz (too passive), trivia (too generic)

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Grid | The 3×3 or 4×4 cell playing field |
| Deck | The shuffled set of player cards dealt each game |
| Wildcard | Power-up allowing placement of any player anywhere (1 per game) |
| Combo cell | A cell requiring a player to satisfy two conditions (e.g., India + MI) |
| Streak | Consecutive correct placements (multiplies score) or consecutive daily plays |
| Daily grid | The seeded grid consistent for all players on a given day |
